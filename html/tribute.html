Prof. Wangari Muta Maathai

"Mother nature"

Social background:

  • born april 1,1940 in Ihithe Village, Tetu Division, Nyeri District, Kenya
  • She married Mwangi Mathai, in May 1969. The couple was blessed with three children(Muta Mathai, Wanjira Mathai, Waweru Mathai). They parted ways in 1977 which was followed by a legal separation in 1979.

Educational background:

  • 1960 - Maathai wins a scholarship in 1960 to go to college in the United States.
  • 1964 - Maathai earns a bachelor's degree in biology in 1964.
  • 1966 - Maathai completes a master's degree in biological sciences at the University of Pittsburgh.
  • 1971- Wangari Maathai receives a Ph.D., effectively becoming the first woman in either East or Central Africa to earn a doctorate.

Some awards and major achievements:

  • She was bestowed with one of France’s most honourable decorations, Legion d’honneur, in 2006.
  • She was awarded two honorary degrees, Doctor of Public Service by the University of Pittsburgh in 2006 and Doctor of Science by Syracuse University posthumously in 2013.

Social impact:

  • 1977 - Maathai launches the Green Belt Movement to reforest her beloved country while helping the nation's women.
  • 1989 - Maathai and her organization stage a protest in Nairobi's Uhuru Park to prevent the construction of a skyscraper. Her campaign drew international attention, and the project was eventually dropped. The place in the park where she demonstrated became known as "Freedom Corner."
  • 1990 - Maathai is beaten and badly injured at another protest in "Freedom Corner."
  • 1992 - Civic and Environmental Education workshops by the Green Belt Movement begin in response to tribal clashes prior to elections.
  • 1998 - Karura Forest privatization protested.
  • 1998 - Green Belt Movement starts planting only indigenous trees.
  • 2004 - Maathai is given the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize for "her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace," according to the Nobel Foundation website.
  • 2011 - Maathai dies on September 25, 2011, at the age of 71 years old.

Famous quotes of her's:

  • "There are opportunities even in the most difficult moments."
  • "Human rights are not things that are put on the table for people to enjoy.These are things you fight for and then you protect."
  • "I'm very conscious of the fact that you cant do it alone . its teamwork. when you do it alone you run the risk that when you are not there nobody else will do it."
